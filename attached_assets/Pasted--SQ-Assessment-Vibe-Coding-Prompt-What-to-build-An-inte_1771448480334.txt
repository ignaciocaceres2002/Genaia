# SQ Assessment — Vibe Coding Prompt

## What to build

An interactive assessment that measures someone's readiness to work with AI — without ever asking about AI. It measures 6 cognitive patterns through everyday scenarios anyone can relate to. It also captures the person's real work tasks to estimate automation potential. The output: an SQ score (0–100), a 6-skill radar, a task automation map, and personalized recommendations. Total time: ~10 minutes in 3 parts. Design: Apple-clean, violet palette (`#7C3AED` primary, `#5B21B6` deep, `#A78BFA` lavender, `#EDE9FE` soft, `#FAFAFE` bg, `#1A1A2E` ink). Full-screen, one element at a time, no clutter, generous animations (Framer Motion). Must feel like a premium personality experience, not a corporate quiz.

---

## The 6 skills being measured

1. **Data Fluency** — Interpreting information critically, questioning sources, understanding probability and confidence
2. **Adaptive Mindset** — Composure with change, learning agility, openness to new tools and methods
3. **Verification Instinct** — Questioning confident sources, detecting errors, not trusting blindly
4. **Orchestration** — Knowing when to delegate, co-create, or override; calibrating trust; briefing effectively
5. **Proactive Experimentation** — Self-directed exploration, building before being asked, tinkering instinct
6. **Systems Redesign** — Thinking from zero, questioning legacy processes, designing new workflows instead of patching old ones

## PART 1: "Instinct" — 12 binary choices (~2 min)

Full-screen, one question at a time. Two large tappable cards per question. No back button. Auto-advance on tap (0.6s delay). 12 dots as progress indicator at top. Response time measured silently.

**Data Fluency:**
Q1: "A chart contradicts your intuition." → "Dig into the data" / "Trust my experience"
Q2: "Weather app says 30% chance of rain." → "Probably won't rain" / "I'm bringing an umbrella"

**Adaptive Mindset:**
Q3: "A tool you've used for years gets replaced." → "Excited to explore" / "Frustrated to relearn"
Q4: "Asked to do something you've never done." → "Energized" / "Anxious"

**Verification Instinct:**
Q5: "GPS says left. Your gut says right." → "Follow the GPS" / "Check the map myself"
Q6: "An expert says something surprising on TV." → "I'd want a second source" / "They're the expert"

**Orchestration:**
Q7: "Someone offers to handle a task you care about." → "Delegate and review" / "Do it myself"
Q8: "A colleague's draft isn't great but has good ideas." → "Build on it" / "Start fresh"

**Proactive Experimentation:**
Q9: "Your afternoon unexpectedly frees up." → "Explore something new" / "Get ahead on existing work"
Q10: "You don't know how to do something." → "Figure it out myself" / "Ask someone who knows"

**Systems Redesign:**
Q11: "A process at work has always been done one way." → "Ask why" / "If it works, leave it"
Q12: "A problem keeps coming back despite fixes." → "Redesign the system" / "Execute the fix better"

### Transition screen after Q12:
"Interesting."
[pause] "We're starting to see your pattern."
[pause] "Now let's go deeper."
Button: "Continue"

## PART 2: "Dilemmas" — 10 scenarios, 4 options each (~5 min)

Full-screen per scenario. Short narrative (3 lines max), then 4 option cards below. Tap to select, others fade, auto-advance. Progress: "3 of 10" top corner. Subtle abstract background shapes shift per scenario.

Every option is valid — no obviously "right" answer. Each maps to 1–3 skills with different weights.

**S1: The Restaurant** (Data Fluency)
Picking a restaurant for a special dinner. A friend swears by a place. An app shows a different one: 4.8 stars, 2,000 reviews.
→ "Go with my friend — personal taste beats ratings"
→ "Go with the app — 2,000 reviews can't be wrong"
→ "Read 10 recent reviews of the app pick to check if they match what I want"
→ "Look up both on Instagram to see real photos"

**S2: The Numbers** (Verification + Data Fluency)
In a meeting. A colleague presents numbers that feel off. Nothing obviously wrong, but your gut flags something. Everyone else is nodding.
→ "Stay quiet — I don't have proof"
→ "Ask: Can you walk me through how we got that number?"
→ "Note it down, check after the meeting"
→ "Say: Something feels off — can we pause on this?"

**S3: The Detour** (Adaptive Mindset)
Driving to something important. Usual route completely blocked. 20 minutes left.
→ "Follow the detour signs"
→ "Open the map, find a completely different route"
→ "Call ahead to say I might be late, then figure it out calmly"
→ "Push through as fast as possible"

**S4: The Deal** (Verification)
Online deal: 80% off something you've wanted. Website looks professional. All 5-star reviews. But the price is way below market.
→ "Buy it — reviews are good, site looks legit"
→ "Google the website name + 'scam'"
→ "Read reviews carefully — look for fake patterns"
→ "Skip it — too good to be true"

**S5: The Delegation** (Orchestration)
Leading a project. One team member is better than you at the critical part. High stakes. You're responsible.
→ "Let them lead it, review everything before it ships"
→ "Let them lead it, trust their expertise fully"
→ "Work on it together — my oversight plus their skill"
→ "Handle it myself — can't afford mistakes"

**S6: The Brief** (Orchestration)
You need someone else to execute a task that matters a lot. How do you brief them?
→ "Step-by-step instructions with regular check-ins"
→ "Explain the goal and constraints, let them figure out the how"
→ "Do a trial run together, then let them go solo"
→ "Show them a great example: make it like this"

**S7: The Broken Process** (Proactive Experimentation + Systems Redesign)
A weekly report everyone does takes 4 hours because of a clunky step. Nobody complains. It's "how it's done."
→ "Spend personal time figuring out how to fix it"
→ "Flag it to my manager"
→ "Build a fix for myself — share it if it works"
→ "Not my problem — focus on my priorities"

**S8: The Tradition** (Systems Redesign)
Weekly all-hands meeting. 200 people. 10 years running. Most check their phones. Leadership says it's "important for culture."
→ "Replace it: async update + monthly real discussion"
→ "Keep it but completely redesign the format"
→ "It has subtle value even if engagement is low"
→ "Run a 4-week experiment: new format vs. old, measure the difference"

**S9: The Free Saturday** (Proactive Experimentation)
Free Saturday, nothing planned. What actually happens?
→ "Deep rabbit hole — YouTube, podcasts, something I'm curious about"
→ "Catch up on things I've been putting off"
→ "Hang out, go out, relax"
→ "Tinker with something — a project, a tool, an idea"

**S10: The Worried Friend** (Adaptive Mindset + Verification)
A friend is genuinely anxious about AI taking their job. Asks: "Should I be scared?"
→ "Some jobs will change. Yours will probably evolve, not disappear."
→ "People have worried about this with every technology."
→ "Look at which parts of your job are routine vs. creative. The routine parts might change."
→ "I don't know enough yet. Let's both look into it."

### Transition after S10: "The Tease"

"This is your mind."
[pause] "Every choice revealed a pattern."
[pause] "One more step to see the full picture."

## THE REVEAL

### Build-up (8 seconds)
Abstract shapes from the tease morph into the 6-axis radar chart. Labels fade in. Chart shrinks and moves to upper section. A large number counts up from 0 to their SQ score (ease-out). Ring draws around it (violet gradient, 1.5s). Level name fades in below. Then: "Top X% of [Role] professionals."

2 seconds of breathing room. No interaction.

Then the full results page scrolls in below.

### Results page (single scrollable page, 7 blocks)

**Block 1 — SQ Score:** Ring + number + level + one-line personality summary combining their top pattern. Example: "You think in patterns, act on instinct, and trust too quickly. That's a superpower with one blind spot."

**Block 2 — Skill Map:** 6-axis radar (violet fill, gray dashed benchmark). Below: 6 horizontal cards. Each: skill name, score, progress bar, one-line SPECIFIC insight (not generic praise). Example — Verification: 41 — "You trust confident sources. AI is confident about everything — even when it's wrong."

**Block 3 — Your Week Decoded:** Horizontal stacked bar (violet = Human Essential, amber = Augmentable, green = Automatable). Three numbers: hours/week recoverable, hours/year, estimated $/year. Then task-by-task cards with category tag + AI opportunity line + drain flag where applicable: "⚡ This drains you. It's 78% automatable."

**Block 4 — Your 3 Unlocks:** Three cards, each: a concrete personalized recommendation combining their weakest skill + most automatable task + drain data. Format: what to free up, what skill to build, which course to take.

**Block 5 — The Fear Bridge:** Only shows if they selected a concern. Takes their stated fear, reframes it using their actual SQ data. Example for "skills becoming irrelevant": "The skills that become irrelevant are the automatable ones — you spend 13h/week on those. The skills that become MORE valuable are where you're already strong. The move: free the 13 hours, invest in the 11 where you're irreplaceable."

**Block 6 — Share Card:** Auto-generated visual card (violet gradient bg, SQ number, mini radar, level, "genesis.ai/sq — What's yours?"). Buttons: "Share on LinkedIn" (pre-written post with their lowest skill mentioned) + "Copy link". Below: "14,382 people have discovered their SQ."

**Block 7 — Next Steps:** Three cards:
- "Start training" → signup/platform, mentions their recommended first course
- "Invite your team" → "5+ colleagues unlock a free Team SQ Dashboard"
- "Book a demo" → enterprise CTA
