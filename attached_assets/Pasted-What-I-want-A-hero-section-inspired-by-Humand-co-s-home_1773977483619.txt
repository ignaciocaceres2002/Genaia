Animation behavior:

The rotating text should fade out and fade in smoothly (crossfade, not a hard switch).
The mockup on the right should have a more interesting entrance: each new mockup slides in slightly from the right or bottom with a subtle scale-up (from 0.95 to 1.0) and opacity fade. The exiting mockup fades out simultaneously.
The transition between items should feel premium — smooth, not rushed. Easing should be ease-out or spring-like.
There should be small dot indicators below the text showing which item is active (6 dots), and users can click a dot to jump to that item.

Mockup visual style:

The mockups should look like actual product screenshots — not wireframes, not flat illustrations. They should feel like a real SaaS dashboard.
Use cards with subtle shadows, clean typography, and the existing color system of the platform (the violet accent colors and light backgrounds already in the app).
The mockups should be rendered as styled components or divs, not static images — so they feel alive and part of the page.
Each mockup should be inside a laptop frame or a browser-window frame with a subtle shadow, slightly rotated/angled for depth (like Humand does with their laptop mockup). Keep the angle subtle — this is not a 3D showcase, just enough to feel premium.

Responsive:

On mobile, the mockup goes below the text. The rotation still works but mockups are full-width.
The headline text should be large and impactful on desktop (think 48-56px for the main headline).

This is the HERO section of genaia.ai. It's the first thing anyone sees. It needs to feel polished, premium, and communicate immediately that this is a serious platform — not a course, not a quiz, not a consulting deck.
